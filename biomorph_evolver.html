About Biomorph Evolver

What are Biomorphs?

Biomorphs are recursive tree-like creatures defined by 9 integer genes, invented by Richard Dawkins in The Blind Watchmaker (1986) to demonstrate how complexity can arise from cumulative selection — no designer required.

How to evolve

  • The parent cell (★, top-left) shows the current creature. Click its ☆ to save it to your Gallery.
  • Click any numbered offspring to breed it — it becomes the new parent and a fresh generation is born.
  • 🎲 Random — jumps to a new random genome.
  • ✴️ Reset — returns to the archetype genome from The Blind Watchmaker.

Gene modes

  • Friendly — 5 intuitive sliders: Cx complexity, Sp spread, Ht height, Ta taper, Sw sprawl. Expanded to 9 genes internally.
  • Original — the raw 9-gene Dawkins genome (g0–g7 + depth). Switching modes only changes the display; the brood is not regenerated.

Mutation controls

  • Turbulence — how many genes mutate per offspring (1 = minimal drift, 8 = wild).
  • Leap — maximum step size per mutated gene (1 = creep, 9 = jump).

Gallery & Fossils

  • 🦴 Fossils — every parent you breed from is automatically recorded here (newest first). Click any fossil to rewind to it.
  • ⭐ Gallery — your personally starred favourites. Star them via ☆ on the parent cell.
  • 📖 Classics — curated preset creatures to use as starting points.
  • ⏬ saves a list or brood to file · ⏫ loads it back · 🗑 clears the list.

Export & navigate

  • 📸 Screenshot — downloads the current brood as a PNG image.
  • ⚛️ Creator — opens the Biomorph Creator in a new tab to sculpt a custom starting genome, then import it here via ⭐ Gallery → ⏫.
