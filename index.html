Biomorphs Suite

Complexity from simplicity, by selection alone.

A faithful recreation of Richard Dawkins's biomorphs from The Blind Watchmaker (1986). Two interactive apps — a hands-on Creator and a generational Evolver — bring his landmark computer experiment to life in your browser.

"I can't see… the biomorphs without experiencing a feeling of wonder that such complexity… can be generated by such a simple recursive procedure." — Richard Dawkins, The Blind Watchmaker (1986)

In 1986, Richard Dawkins sparked an idea that would forever change the way we understand biological complexity. His biomorphs program—originally written in Pascal and run on a humble Macintosh—demonstrated something both profound and unsettling: that organic-looking, intricate, and surprisingly beautiful structures could emerge from just a handful of integer genes and a recursive algorithm, without any designer, without any purpose, and without any teleological guidance. Only cumulative selection.

Four decades later, that seminal experiment remains one of the most elegant and pedagogically powerful computational demonstrations of evolutionary theory. To celebrate the 40th anniversary of this concept, we present Biomorphs Suite: an interactive web-based reimplementation faithful to Dawkins’ original 9-gene model (1986), developed for educational and experimental purposes.

The suite includes an “Evolver” mode that preserves the essence of Dawkins’s original framework while enhancing usability through a simplified 5-parameter interface for rapid exploration. It also features a library of classic evolved forms and a manual “Creator” mode, allowing users to design a biomorph manually and use it as the seed for the evolutionary process. The entire application runs client-side, requiring no installation or server overhead.

Biomorph Evolver
Generational selection

Pick survivors, generation after generation. Each click selects an offspring that becomes the new parent — watch creatures drift, converge, or leap into unexpected forms across evolutionary time.

  • 3×3, 1×3, 1×5, 5×5 brood sizes
